# Pricing Options

How to structure and present pricing in proposals. Default: Good/Better/Best tiers.

---

## Why Three Options Work

- **Single price = take it or leave it.** The client's only decision is yes or no.
- **Three options = which one?** The decision shifts from "should I?" to "which one?" — a much easier yes.
- **The middle option wins 60-70% of the time.** That's where you put your ideal project scope.
- **The premium option anchors high.** Even if nobody picks it, it makes the middle feel reasonable.

## Pricing Gap Guidelines

| User's Target Price | Option A | Option B (Target) | Option C |
|--------------------|----------|-------------------|----------|
| $1,000 | $700 | $1,000 | $1,800 |
| $2,500 | $1,800 | $2,500 | $4,500 |
| $5,000 | $3,500 | $5,000 | $8,500 |
| $10,000 | $7,000 | $10,000 | $16,000 |

**Rule of thumb:**
- Option A = 70% of target price
- Option B = target price (mark as RECOMMENDED)
- Option C = 170-200% of target price

---

## Naming the Options

**Don't use:** "Basic / Standard / Premium" — sounds like a SaaS pricing page, not a custom proposal.

**Better names by industry:**

| Industry | Option A | Option B | Option C |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| Marketing | Foundation | Growth | Domination |
| Web Development | Launch | Complete | Enterprise |
| Consulting | Strategy | Strategy + Execution | Full Partnership |
| Coaching | Essentials | Accelerator | VIP |
| Design | Starter | Full Brand | Brand System |
| General | Core | Recommended | Premium |

---

## What Differentiates Each Tier

**Option A (Good):** The minimum viable project. Solves the core problem but leaves room for "I wish I'd gotten more."
- Core deliverables only
- Fewer revisions
- Standard timeline
- No ongoing support

**Option B (Better = Target):** The complete solution. Everything they need, nothing they don't.
- All core deliverables + key extras
- More revisions
- Priority timeline
- Some support included
- **Mark as RECOMMENDED**

**Option C (Best):** The premium experience. For clients who want everything handled.
- Everything in B + premium add-ons
- Unlimited revisions (within scope)
- Fastest timeline
- Extended support
- Strategy or consulting component
- Done-for-you vs done-with-you

## Payment Terms

### Common Structures

| Structure | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|
| 50% upfront, 50% on completion | Most common for projects under $5K |
| 33/33/33 (start, midpoint, completion) | Projects $5K-15K |
| Monthly retainer | Ongoing services |
| 100% upfront | Small projects under $1K, or premium positioning |
| Net 30 | Enterprise/corporate clients (they'll expect it) |

### Payment Terms Copy

```
PAYMENT SCHEDULE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
{{Percentage}}% (${{amount}}) due upon agreement — to begin Phase 1
{{Percentage}}% (${{amount}}) due at {{milestone}} — to begin Phase 2
{{Percentage}}% (${{amount}}) due upon delivery of final deliverables

Payment accepted via: {{methods — bank transfer, credit card, PayPal, etc.}}
```

## Single Price Format

When the user doesn't want tiers:

```
INVESTMENT
━━━━━━━━━━
Project Total: ${{price}}

This includes:
- {{All deliverables listed}}
- {{Revisions policy}}
- {{Support/timeline details}}

Payment: {{terms}}
```

> **AGENT: Even when the user wants a single price, consider asking:**
> "Would you like me to include an optional add-on or upgrade for clients who want more? It can increase your average deal size without adding a full tier."

## Non-Service Proposal Pricing

> **AGENT: Not all proposal types use Good/Better/Best the same way.**

### Membership Proposals
Tiers map naturally — frame as access levels, not project scope:

| Tier | Frame As | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Option A | Community Access | Online community + monthly content |
| Option B | Full Member | Community + group coaching + resources |
| Option C | VIP / Inner Circle | Everything + 1:1 access + priority support |

Name options: Community / Member / VIP, or Bronze / Silver / Gold

Payment: Monthly or annual recurring — show both with annual discount.

### Sponsorship Proposals
Use level-based naming:

| Level | Typical Range | Includes |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| 🥉 Bronze | $500-2,000 | Logo placement, newsletter mention |
| 🥈 Silver | $2,000-5,000 | Above + booth, social feature |
| 🥇 Gold | $5,000-15,000 | Above + speaking slot, banner, email feature |
| 👑 Title | $15,000+ | Above + name in title, keynote intro, VIP |

### Speaking Proposals
Typically single fee + expenses, but can offer tiers:

| Package | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Keynote Only | 45-60 min talk + Q&A |
| Keynote + Workshop | Talk + half-day hands-on session |
| Full Day | Keynote + workshop + consulting/strategy session |

Always separate fee from travel/accommodation expenses.

### Partnership Proposals
Tiers usually don't apply. Instead, present the partnership structure with clear mutual commitments. If there are partnership levels (affiliate vs co-brand vs joint venture), present them as distinct models, not price tiers.
