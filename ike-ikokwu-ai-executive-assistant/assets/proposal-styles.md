# Proposal Styles

5 visual and tonal styles for proposals. Select based on the client's industry, the project type, and the user's brand.

---

## Style Selection Guide

| Style | Best For | Tone | Template |
|-------|----------|------|----------|
| Corporate | Enterprise, law, finance, government, healthcare | Formal, structured, traditional | `42-law-firm-professional` |
| Consultant | B2B services, marketing, strategy, coaching | Professional, approachable, warm | `03-light-professional-coach` |
| Creative | Design, branding, media, startups, tech | Modern, visual, personality-driven | `30-creative-agency` |
| Minimal | SaaS, dev, technical services, efficiency-focused | Clean, no fluff, data-driven | `09-modern-startup-minimal` |
| Bold | High-ticket, luxury, premium services, speaking | Dark, gold accents, high perceived value | `01-bold-dark-authority` |

---

## 1. Corporate

**When to use:** Enterprise clients, government contracts, legal/financial services, healthcare. Clients who expect formality and thoroughness.

**Tone guide:**
- Third person preferred ("Our team will..." not "I'll...")
- Formal language ("We propose" not "Here's what we'll do")
- Complete sentences, no fragments
- Minimal use of contractions
- Section headers are descriptive, not clever
- Numbers and data emphasized

**Formatting:**
- Clear section numbering (1.0, 1.1, 1.2)
- Tables for timelines and deliverables
- Cover page with company logos (if available)
- Standard business letter conventions

---

## 2. Consultant

**When to use:** B2B services, marketing agencies, strategy consultants, coaches working with businesses. The "trusted advisor" feel.

**Tone guide:**
- First person is fine ("I'll..." or "We'll...")
- Professional but warm — like talking to a colleague you respect
- Contractions are okay ("you'll," "we're," "that's")
- Balance confidence with approachability
- Use "you" more than "we" — focus on their outcomes

**Formatting:**
- Clean headers, not numbered
- Bulleted lists for deliverables and scope
- Conversational executive summary
- Personal sign-off at the end

---

## 3. Creative

**When to use:** Design agencies, branding projects, media companies, startups, tech companies. Clients who value creativity and originality.

**Tone guide:**
- Personality is welcome — show your brand voice
- Shorter sentences, punchy language
- Can use em dashes, parenthetical asides, and informal phrasing
- Bold claims backed by results
- Less formal structure — can break conventions if it serves the message

**Formatting:**
- Visual hierarchy matters more than rigid structure
- Larger text for key points
- Can include portfolio references or project previews
- Bold use of color and whitespace

---

## 4. Minimal

**When to use:** SaaS, developer services, technical consulting, efficiency-obsessed clients. People who hate fluff.

**Tone guide:**
- Direct. No preamble, no fluff, no "we're thrilled to present"
- Lead with the facts
- Bullet points over paragraphs
- Numbers, metrics, and specifics dominate
- If you can say it in 5 words, don't use 15

**Formatting:**
- Maximum whitespace
- Sparse, scannable layout
- Tables over prose
- Executive summary can be 3 bullet points instead of paragraphs

---

## 5. Bold

**When to use:** High-ticket services ($5K+), luxury clients, premium positioning, speaking/keynote proposals, exclusive offerings.

**Tone guide:**
- Confident, authoritative, premium
- "Investment" not "cost" or "price"
- Understated rather than overselling — let the credentials speak
- Selective language — "We work with a limited number of clients who..."
- No discounts, no "deals" — the premium is the point

**Formatting:**
- Dark backgrounds, gold or light accents
- Serif fonts for headlines (if HTML)
- Generous spacing
- Testimonials from recognizable names or titles
- Clean, magazine-quality feel

---

## Automatic Style Selection

> **AGENT: If the user doesn't specify a style, select based on these signals:**
>
> | Signal | Suggested Style |
> |--------|----------------|
> | Client is a corporation or enterprise | Corporate |
> | User is a solo consultant or small agency | Consultant |
> | Project involves design, branding, or creative work | Creative |
> | Client is a tech company or startup | Minimal |
> | Project is $5,000+ and user has strong credentials | Bold |
> | User says "keep it simple" | Minimal |
> | User says "make it look premium" | Bold |
>
> When in doubt, default to **Consultant** — it works for the broadest range of situations.
